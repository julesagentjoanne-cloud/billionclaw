# Competitor Ad Intelligence

## Purpose
Define how startupClaw should use competitor ads as market intelligence for messaging, creative strategy, and campaign decision-making.

## Simple overview
Competitor ads should be used as signal, not as copy templates.

They help answer:
- what messages competitors keep repeating
- which pain points they are emphasizing
- which offers and proof mechanisms they use
- where the market is saturated
- where whitespace may exist

## Primary sources
- Facebook Ad Library
- LinkedIn sponsored content / ad visibility
- competitor landing pages tied to ads
- repeat exposure over time

## What to capture per competitor
- brand / company
- ad date / observed window
- platform
- target persona guess
- pain point
- core promise
- proof style
- CTA
- creative format
- offer type
- repetition frequency
- linked landing page
- notes on strategic theme

## Core rule
Repeated ads are stronger signals than one-off ads.
If a competitor keeps using the same message, angle, or proof structure, assume it may be surviving internal performance scrutiny.

## How startupClaw should use this
### 1. Messaging intelligence
- identify common category claims
- identify overused phrases and saturated angles
- identify promising but underdeveloped positioning territory

### 2. Creative input
- feed patterns into [[startupClaw - Action Modules - Creative Strategy]]
- help shape angle buckets, proof structures, and creative formats

### 3. Kill/scale context
- use market ad repetition as one supporting signal when deciding whether to persist with or refresh an internal test
- do not override internal performance data with competitor activity alone

### 4. Market learning loop
- monitor how competitor creative evolves over time
- track whether category language is shifting
- identify when a previously niche angle becomes mainstream

## Deliverables
- competitor ad tracker
- messaging pattern summary
- proof/offer pattern summary
- whitespace opportunity notes
- creative strategy snapshot

## Guardrails
- do not blindly copy competitor ads
- do not assume visible ads are profitable ads
- use competitor signals as context, not truth
