# Domain Specialist Perspectives

## Purpose
Force startupClaw to test each startup idea from multiple real-world roles inside the domain instead of relying on one flattened view.

## Simple overview
Every startup idea should be reviewed through at least 3 domain perspectives:
- **end-user**
- **manager**
- **executive**

These can be different people or the same person wearing different hats.

## Why it matters
A startup can look strong from one perspective and weak from another.

Examples:
- end-user may love it
- manager may see rollout friction
- executive may not see enough ROI

Or:
- executive loves the efficiency story
- manager loves the visibility/control story
- end-user resists the workflow change

## Core rule
For every idea, startupClaw should ask:
1. What does the end-user care about?
2. What does the manager/operator care about?
3. What does the executive/budget owner care about?
4. Where do those views align?
5. Where do they conflict?

## Typical role lens
### End-user
- daily pain
- friction
- usability
- personal win/loss

### Manager
- workflow fit
- team adoption
- visibility/control
- implementation burden

### Executive
- ROI
- strategic value
- budget logic
- risk/trust

## Rule for startupClaw
This should be treated as a core startup-generation and GTM mechanic.
Do not assume one persona is enough when the buying/use system is multi-layered.

When possible, startupClaw should reason through these perspectives with expert-grade domain understanding, not generic role descriptions.
