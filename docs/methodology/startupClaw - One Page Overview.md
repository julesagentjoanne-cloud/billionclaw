# startupClaw - One Page Overview

## Purpose
Keep the top layer of startupClaw simple enough that founders and internal users can immediately understand what it does, what they put in, what they get out, and what happens next.

## What startupClaw is
startupClaw helps a startup move forward from its **current state**.

It does this by:
- understanding what the startup is building
- understanding what problem it is trying to solve
- researching likely audiences, pain points, and alternatives
- identifying the likely startup stage
- recommending the best next GTM move
- coordinating the right work modules so progress does not stop at diagnosis

startupClaw should feel like a simple front-end to an orchestrated swarm of specialized work:
- research
- validation
- messaging
- offer design
- growth execution
- onboarding / retention
- operator systems

## What the founder puts in
Very simple starting input:
- a short business statement via [[startupClaw - Business Statement Mode]]

Optional if they know it:
- current alternatives
- why they think people would pay
- what proof they already have
- deeper details from [[startupClaw - Input Template]] when needed

## What startupClaw gives back
A simple founder-facing snapshot and, when useful, a fuller startup intelligence report via [[startupClaw - Full Report Template]]:
- what you are likely building
- who likely cares most
- what urgent pain likely matters most
- what alternatives exist today
- why they may pay
- what is evidence vs assumption
- what startup stage you are likely in
- what is likely blocking progress
- what the best next move is
- which execution agent can help next

## How startupClaw helps
It is designed to create an early aha-moment.

A good output should help the founder feel:
- I understand who likely has the pain
- I understand what still needs validation
- I understand what stage I am in
- I know what to do next
- I have a system that can help do the next useful work too

## Stage-based recommendation logic
startupClaw should tailor recommendations to the startup stage.

### 1. Problem discovery
Typical need:
- confirm the problem is real and painful

Likely recommendations:
- run customer interviews
- sharpen ICP hypotheses
- validate urgency and pain language
- confirm that the problem matters enough to solve

### 2. Solution validation
Typical need:
- confirm the proposed solution is desirable enough

Likely recommendations:
- test solution interest with real users
- refine offer and positioning
- validate willingness to pay
- identify strongest audience/pain combination

### 3. Early revenue
Typical need:
- confirm the startup can generate revenue in reality

Likely recommendations:
- tighten offer/pricing
- improve conversion path
- focus on a small number of high-signal customers
- remove friction between interest and purchase

### 4. Repeatable acquisition
Typical need:
- make growth more reliable

Likely recommendations:
- identify the best repeatable channel
- improve messaging and conversion
- measure funnel performance better
- reduce overreliance on founder heroics

### 5. Scalable growth
Typical need:
- grow faster without breaking the system

Likely recommendations:
- improve operational leverage
- strengthen measurement and decision loops
- expand channels carefully
- reduce bottlenecks in retention, operations, or founder dependency

## Optional preview of what comes next
Show this only if the user wants more detail.

### What startupClaw can do next
- deeper startup stage assessment
- bottleneck diagnosis
- GTM component grading
- 30 / 60 / 90 day action path
- more detailed audience/pain research
- more detailed next-step roadmap
- generate execution assets (scripts, drafts, checklists, test plans)
- let the founder choose a buyable execution agent from [[startupClaw - Buyable Agent Catalog]]
- review what changed and update the diagnosis

## Rule
Keep the default experience simple:
- short input
- short snapshot
- one best next move

Keep the deeper system optional:
- show a preview of what can come next
- only expand when the founder wants more detail or the startup needs deeper diagnosis
