# startupClaw

## Purpose
A startup-focused system that helps founders go from a rough startup idea to a clearer audience, pain point, startup stage, and next GTM move.

## Simple overview
startupClaw starts with a lightweight founder input:
- what are you building?
- what problem are you solving?

Then it expands that input with research, suggests likely audiences and pains, highlights what is still assumption vs evidence, and guides the founder toward the next best validation or GTM step.

## Core idea
Do not force the founder into a heavy questionnaire immediately.

Instead:
1. capture the startup thesis
2. research around it
3. suggest likely audiences/pains/alternatives
4. identify the current startup stage
5. guide the founder into the next best diagnostic or GTM step

## Flow
### 1. Founder thesis input
- startup description
- problem solved

### 2. Research expansion
- gather external evidence
- suggest likely audience options
- suggest likely pain points
- suggest alternatives / competitors

### 3. Validation pressure test
- what is evidence vs assumption?
- what seems likely but unproven?
- what should be tested next?

### 4. Stage and bottleneck mapping
- identify likely startup stage
- identify likely bottleneck
- identify highest-leverage next move

### 5. GTM setup guidance
- suggest the next channel / motion / validation step
- only move deeper into the full intake when needed

## Why it matters
startupClaw is the fast aha-moment layer before the heavier diagnostic system.

It helps the founder realize things like:
- who likely has the strongest pain
- what assumptions are still weak
- where they are in the startup journey
- what to do next instead of feeling stuck everywhere

Longer term, startupClaw should evolve from a smart intake/diagnosis layer into an orchestrated operator system that helps coordinate the work needed to actually move the startup forward.

## Relationship to the broader system
startupClaw is not the whole GTM advisory system.
It is an entry layer inside the broader startup growth system.

## Detailed context
startupClaw should behave like a hypothesis builder, not just a form.

That means it should:
- use founder input as a starting point
- expand the picture with research
- present structured options instead of dumping research
- guide the user into validation and GTM setup in a staged way

A strong startupClaw output might include:
- likely target audiences
- likely pain points
- likely alternatives used today
- likely willingness-to-pay signals
- biggest unknowns
- best next question
- likely startup stage
- suggested GTM next step
