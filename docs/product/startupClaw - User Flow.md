# startupClaw - User Flow

## Purpose
Define the founder-facing user flow for startupClaw so it can create a fast aha-moment, then guide the user into deeper diagnosis and GTM setup.

## Simple overview
startupClaw should feel like this:
1. founder explains what they are building and what problem they solve
2. startupClaw researches the space
3. startupClaw suggests likely audiences, pains, and alternatives
4. founder confirms or corrects those suggestions
5. startupClaw identifies likely stage, bottleneck, and next GTM step
6. only then does it move into deeper intake if needed

## User flow

### Step 1. Founder thesis input
Goal:
- get the startup thesis quickly without heavy friction

Prompt types:
- what are you building?
- what problem are you solving?
- who do you think this is for? (optional)
- what alternatives do you think exist? (optional)

Output:
- initial founder thesis
- first hypothesis set

### Step 2. Research expansion
Goal:
- enrich the founder thesis with external signal

Use:
- [[startupClaw - Research Inputs]]
- [[Pain Point Research Sources]]

Research output should surface:
- likely audience options
- likely pain points
- likely alternatives / competitors
- willingness-to-pay clues
- exact language patterns

Output:
- AI-suggested audience set
- AI-suggested pain set
- evidence snippets
- confidence level

### Step 3. Guided founder selection
Goal:
- let the user refine direction without needing to invent everything from scratch

startupClaw should ask things like:
- which audience sounds closest?
- which pain feels most urgent?
- which alternative is the closest substitute today?
- which suggested wording feels most accurate?

Output:
- sharper audience hypothesis
- sharper pain hypothesis
- sharper competitive context

### Step 4. Validation pressure test
Goal:
- separate beliefs from evidence

Questions:
- what do you already know versus what do you believe?
- what proof do you have?
- do you have customer conversations, usage, waitlist, revenue, or other signal?
- what is still unvalidated?

Output:
- evidence-backed vs unvalidated assumptions
- biggest unknowns
- validation priority list

### Step 5. Startup stage mapping
Goal:
- place the founder in the startup journey

Possible stage outputs:
- problem discovery
- solution validation
- early revenue
- repeatable acquisition
- scalable growth

Output:
- likely stage
- reason for the stage call

### Step 6. Bottleneck diagnosis
Goal:
- identify what is actually blocking progress

Use:
- [[Startup Assessment Scorecard]]
- [[Revenue Growth Diagnostic System]]
- [[Growth Bottleneck Framework]]

Possible outputs:
- demand bottleneck
- positioning bottleneck
- offer bottleneck
- acquisition bottleneck
- conversion bottleneck
- retention bottleneck
- measurement bottleneck
- sequencing bottleneck

Output:
- likely primary bottleneck
- likely secondary bottleneck
- strongest area
- weakest area

### Step 7. GTM next-step guidance
Goal:
- give the founder a practical next move, not just diagnosis

Possible outputs:
- validate audience X with 10 interviews
- test offer/pricing angle Y
- sharpen positioning around pain Z
- test channel A first
- fix conversion friction before adding traffic
- improve activation before scaling acquisition

Output:
- best next move
- why this move matters now
- what success signal to watch

### Step 8. Deep-dive path when needed
Goal:
- only move into the heavier process if the startup needs more precision

Trigger conditions:
- too many unknowns remain
- the founder wants a fuller diagnosis
- the startup is complex enough to justify deeper intake
- enough evidence exists to warrant detailed scorecarding

Deep-dive tools:
- [[Startup Intake Questionnaire]]
- [[Startup Assessment Scorecard]]
- [[GTM Component Grading Model]]

Output:
- deeper assessment
- 30 / 60 / 90 day action path

## Product design rule
startupClaw should not feel like a form.
It should feel like a guided startup-thinking companion.

That means:
- fast progress
- structured suggestions
- visible reasoning
- clear distinction between assumptions and evidence
- one best next step instead of too many options

## Aha-moment design rule
A good early startupClaw output should make the founder feel:
- “I now see who likely cares most.”
- “I now see what still needs validation.”
- “I now understand where I am in the startup journey.”
- “I know the next GTM move.”
