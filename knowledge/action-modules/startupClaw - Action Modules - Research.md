# startupClaw - Action Modules - Research

## Purpose
Define research-oriented action modules startupClaw can execute to gather faster market signal.

## Simple overview
Use these modules when the startup needs better evidence about:
- audience
- pain points
- alternatives
- market language
- competitor gaps

## Action modules
### 1. Audience research
- identify likely audience segments
- compare segment pain intensity
- surface likely best-fit segment
- output a simple segment comparison with evidence strength, not just a list of segments

### 2. Pain-point research
- gather forum/community pain language
- gather review-site dissatisfaction patterns
- identify urgency triggers and workaround behavior
- separate chronic annoyance from urgent problem pressure so later agents do not overrate weak pain

### 3. Alternative / competitor research
- identify direct competitors
- identify indirect workarounds
- identify competitor gaps and complaints
- extract where users are settling, paying, switching, or stitching together workarounds

### 4. Language mining
- extract exact phrasing people use for the problem
- identify objections and emotional language
- identify desired outcomes in customer words
- tag language by audience, pain stage, and buying context where possible

### 5. Research confidence scoring
- label each conclusion as strong signal, moderate signal, weak signal, or open question
- note what evidence would be needed to upgrade or invalidate the conclusion

### 6. Research-to-next-step routing
- route to validation when evidence is interesting but not reliable enough to act on
- route to messaging when the main value is usable customer language and angle clarity
- route to offer work when pain and willingness-to-pay clues are strong enough to shape packaging or pricing
- route to GTM execution only when the evidence is specific enough to support real channel or execution choices

## Product UI mapping note
These modules should map into product UI as:
- a research workspace with evidence buckets
- a segment comparison view
- a pain/urgency snapshot
- a confidence label on each major conclusion
- an explicit next-step routing card

## Best use cases
- problem discovery
- weak evidence quality
- unclear ICP
- unclear pain intensity
